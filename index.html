Research field.

HCI · CREATIVE DESIGN · GENAI
4 PROCESS  ·  4 AREAS

I build the interactive scaffolding that lets designers and generative AI hand intent back and forth — without losing what made it the designer's in the first place.

— capturing a designer's intent, decomposing the design into operable parts, structuring the relations between them, and generating outputs that hand the reasoning back to the designer to steer the next move.

— Process how my work moves, end to end
/ capture

Capturing Designer Intent

Two routes — the explicit (direct manipulation, semantic tags) and the implicit (accumulated behaviors, gaze, dwell). Both feed a shared internal representation.

direct manipulationbehavioral logssemantic tagging
/ decompose

Operable Decomposition

Breaking a design into parts a designer can actually grab — across text, 2D image, 3D shape, and multimodal channels. Semantic and structural decomposition working in tandem.

semanticstructuralmultimodal
/ structure

Structuring Element Relations

A relational graph of design elements that evolves turn by turn — chair → frame → wood → sustainable → bamboo — building a map that the designer can re-enter later.

relational graphturn-basedevolution
/ generate

Generation & Interaction

Closing the loop. Outputs that expose property edges ("is-style-of", "has-property") so designers can reach into the model's reasoning and steer the next iteration.

property graphsteerableiterative
— Research areas themes my work returns to
/ 01

Interactive Design Space Exploration

Tools that help designers traverse, narrow, and rediscover regions of a generative latent space — without losing the thread of their own intent.

/ 02

Intent Alignment with GenAI

Mediating implicit and explicit signals between human and model, so what the designer means and what the model produces converge over time.

/ 03

Personalized Cognitive Maps

Externalizing the designer's unique semantic geometry — color, material, form — as a map that grows with use, supporting reflection and re-use.

/ 04

UX in Individual Cognitive Space

Studying how interaction shapes the cognitive scaffolding of a single user, and how interfaces can adapt to that geometry rather than flatten it.
